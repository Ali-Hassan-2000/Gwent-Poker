Poker Rules

Poker is a card game where players make bets based on the rank and strength of their hands. The goal is to win the pot, which is the sum of all bets placed during a hand. Key elements include hand rankings, betting rounds, and the showdown.

Gameplay:

  • Dealing: Cards are dealt to each player, either face-down (hole cards) or face-up (community cards).
  • Betting Rounds: Players take turns betting, with options to call (match a bet), raise (increase the bet), or fold (give up on the hand).
  • Showdown: If more than one player remains after the final betting round, they reveal their hands, and the player with the best hand wins the pot.

Hand Rankings:

  • Royal Flush: Ace, King, Queen, Jack, Ten, all of the same suit.
  • Straight Flush: Five cards in sequence, all of the same suit.
  • Four of a Kind: Four cards of the same rank.
  • Full House: Three of a kind and a pair.
  • Flush: Five cards of the same suit.
  • Straight: Five cards in sequence (Ace can be high or low).
  • Three of a Kind: Three cards of the same rank.
  • Two Pair: Two sets of pairs.
  • One Pair: Two cards of the same rank.
  • High Card: The highest card in the hand, if no other combinations are formed.

Betting:

  • Check: If no bet has been made, a player can check, meaning they pass the action to the next player.
  • Call: Matching the current bet.
  • Raise: Increasing the current bet.
  • Fold: Giving up on the hand and forfeiting any bets placed.
